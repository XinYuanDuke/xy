Research Interests:

Signal Processing, Machine Learning, Applied Statistics, Compressive Sensing, Computer Vision, Computational Photography, Image and Video Processing

My research in Duke lies in the following different directions:

  1. Recent research with Prof. Lawrence Carin focus on the applied statistics, by developing structured shrinkage priors with applications in machine learning, compressive sensing and sparse coding. We are also developing various Bayesian frameworks for classification and clustering.
  2. Develop Bayesian reconstruction algorithms for the compressive X-ray scatter imaging and compressive X-ray computed tomography with Prof. Robert Calderbank, Prof. Lawrence Carin and Prof. David Brady in the coded aperture X-ray imaging (CAXI) project.
  3. Developed advanced "leave-bag-behind" detection algorithm with Prof. Guillermo Sapiro, Prof. Lawrence Carin for the Department of Homeland Security (DHS).
  4. Since May, 2012 in Duke University, I focus on the coded-aperture video compressive sensing (CACTI) and hyperspectral compressive sensing (CASSI) technique, working with Prof. David Brady, Prof. Lawrence Carin and Prof. Guillermo Sapiro including the hardware development, reconstruction algorithms (both Bayesian and optimization, both global and local dictionary learning) and theoretical analysis.
  5. I am also developing Bayesian models for biomedical applications, to analyze the gene data with Lovelace Respiratory Research Institute.

During my Ph.D in Hong Kong Polytechnic University (2009/09 ~ 2012/03), I was working on sensor array signal processing, including direction-of-arrival estimation, beamforming, polarization estimation, interference suppression and signal detection, with focus on diversely polarized antenna arrays (Electromagnetic vector-sensor), and acoustic vector-sensor arrays.

When I was a master student in Xidian University (2007~2009), I developed several prototypes of radar signal processing systems and other electronic products with A/D, D/A converter and DSP, FPGA, ARM, MCU electronic chips.
